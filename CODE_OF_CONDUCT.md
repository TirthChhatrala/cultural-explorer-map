# Code of Conduct

> **Cultural Explorer Map** — An AI-powered platform for cultural discovery, travel planning, storytelling, and exploration across India's rich heritage.

This Code of Conduct governs participation in the Cultural Explorer Map community across all project spaces, including the GitHub repository, discussions, pull requests, issues, and any events or communications where contributors represent the project.

## 1. Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and leaders of the Cultural Explorer Map community, pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone.

We are committed to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity and national origin
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience or education
- Socio-economic status
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We pledge to act and interact in ways that uphold these values at every level of participation.

## 2. Our Standards

### 2.1 Expected Behaviour

The following behaviours are expected of all community members and contribute to a positive, constructive environment:

- **Demonstrate empathy and kindness** toward other contributors, users, and community members
- **Respect differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences** — disagreement is natural; disrespect is not
- **Give and receive constructive feedback gracefully** — critique the work, not the person
- **Take responsibility for mistakes** — acknowledge errors, apologise to those affected, and learn from the experience
- **Act in the best interest of the community** — prioritise collective wellbeing over individual preferences
- **Maintain professional communication** in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and code reviews

---

### 2.2 Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated in any project space:

- Use of sexualised language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting remarks, derogatory comments, or personal and political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any community member
- Publishing another person's private information — such as a physical address, email address, or other identifying details — without their explicit consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community events
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## 3. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all official community spaces associated with Cultural Explorer Map, including:

- The [GitHub repository](https://github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map)
- [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map/issues)
- [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map/discussions)
- [Pull Requests](https://github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map/pulls)
- Code review comments and commit messages
- Any online or offline event where an individual officially represents the Cultural Explorer Map project

This Code also applies when a community member is representing the project in external public spaces — for example, using an official project email address, posting from an official social media account, or acting as a designated project representative at an event.

## 4. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying, upholding, and enforcing the standards described in this Code of Conduct.

Community leaders have the authority and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, wiki edits, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Communicate the reasons for moderation decisions clearly and appropriately
- Take prompt, fair, and proportionate corrective action in response to violations
- Protect the privacy and security of anyone who reports an incident

---

## 5. Reporting a Violation

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainer through the following channel:

- **GitHub Repository:** [github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map](https://github.com/TirthChhatrala/cultural-explorer-map)
- **Direct contact:** Reach the project maintainer privately via GitHub

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The privacy and security of the reporter will be respected at all times. Community leaders are obligated not to disclose reporter identity without explicit consent.

When reporting an incident, please include:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Description** | A clear account of what occurred |
| **Date and Time** | When the incident took place |
| **Location** | The specific community space where it occurred (e.g., issue number, PR link) |
| **Individuals Involved** | Usernames or identifiers of those involved |
| **Evidence** | Screenshots, links, or other supporting material |

## 6. Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will apply the following graduated enforcement framework when determining consequences for violations. Responses will be proportionate to the nature, severity, and impact of the behaviour.

---

### Level 1 — Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or behaviour deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from a community leader that explains the nature of the violation and why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested where appropriate.

---

### Level 2 — Warning

**Community Impact:** A single significant incident or a pattern of minor violations.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with defined consequences for continued behaviour. The individual must cease interaction with those involved — including unsolicited contact through external channels such as social media — for a specified period of time. Violating the terms of a warning may result in a temporary or permanent ban.

---

### Level 3 — Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour or escalation after a prior warning.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from all community interaction and public communication for a defined period. No public or private contact with those involved, including those enforcing this Code of Conduct, is permitted during the ban period. Violating the terms of a temporary ban may result in a permanent ban.

---

### Level 4 — Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** A pattern of repeated violations, sustained harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of groups of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from all forms of public participation in the Cultural Explorer Map community.
